## The Disappearances

Portland has been quietly bleeding people for years. Not deaths --
disappearances. People who were here and then were not. The official explanation
is the housing crisis, the fentanyl crisis, voluntary relocation, the general
churn of a city's mobile population.

The official count is incomplete. The unhoused community in Old Town keeps its
own count, passed mouth to ear. It is significantly higher.

---

## The Numbers

Officially: approximately 40-60 persons reported missing per year in the
Portland metro area, the majority resolved within 90 days. Persistent missing
persons: 12-18 annually. The city's rate is not anomalous by national standards.

The Old Town count: maintained informally by a rotating group of long-term
residents who have been tracking this since roughly 2014. They count people they
knew, specifically -- not strangers passing through, but neighbors. People with
spots, relationships, routines. Their count for the past five years runs about
2.5 times the official rate for their specific geography.

The discrepancy is not mysterious in one sense: unhoused individuals are
chronically undercounted in official missing persons reports. Family members who
might file are often out of contact. Many have outstanding warrants. The system
is not built to track them.

The discrepancy may also mean something else.

---

## The Pattern

The Old Town community has identified, over years of comparison and argument,
some features of the disappearances they track:

**Clustering by season.** More disappearances in October-November and again in
February-March. The Festival month of June has historically been quiet, though
2024 was an exception.

**Location.** Concentrated near the waterfront south of Burnside and in the
blocks immediately east of the underground entrance at NW 3rd. The park blocks
have had several. Forest Park's trailheads on the west side are represented, but
those are attributed to other causes by people in other neighborhoods.

**Profile.** Most are long-term Old Town residents. Several had existing
arrangements with Night-affiliated individuals or businesses -- not formal
agreements, but the kind of informal accommodation that accumulates in a
neighborhood. The concentration of people with Night connections may mean the
Night is a vector, or may mean they are specifically targeted by whoever is
responsible.

Nobody has written this analysis down anywhere the wrong people might read it.
It exists in conversation only.

## What the Factions Think

**The Foundation** believes the disappearances are a symptom of faction
instability -- specifically, that the 60-year-old Night/Power border agreement
is degrading and people are being caught in the resulting friction. They are
monitoring and not acting, which is their institutional response to most things.

**The Night** is certain that some of their people have been taken by the Church
Network. They have evidence for three specific cases. They are nearly done
collecting information before they act. They have been nearly done for eight
months.

**The Church Network** acknowledges three terminations in Old Town in the past
year. They do not consider these disappearances. They consider them operations.
They are aware that the Night's count includes individuals the Network did not
touch, and this troubles some of them. Gerald Fitch has a list he reviews
periodically. He is not sure all of it can be attributed to sanctioned
operations.

**The Wild** is not tracking the disappearances specifically but has noted that
the pattern in Forest Park is separate from the Old Town pattern and may have a
different cause. They have not communicated this to anyone.

**The Portland Thorn** is working a story. They think it's about city contract
fraud. Their source inside the Foundation has been pointing them toward the
disappearance data for eight months. They haven't connected the thread yet. They
are close.

## Rachel Voss

The highest-profile case is [Rachel Voss](npcs/rachel-voss), the Forest Park
eco-resort project manager who was reported missing eight months ago. Her
disappearance is listed as voluntary adult departure. It is the only case that
has received sustained press attention, because she was not unhoused and because
her colleagues at the development firm have money and made noise.

Her family does not believe the voluntary departure classification. They are
right not to believe it. What actually happened to her is not public
information.

---

## The Three Night-Affiliated Missing

Three individuals connected to Night-affiliated businesses in Old Town have
disappeared in the last year. The Night's position is that the Church Network is
responsible for at least two of them and possibly all three.

The three: a bartender at a bar on NW Davis who had worked there for eleven
years. A man who ran a late-night locksmith business out of a van -- origin of
the van unknown, clients mostly nocturnal. A woman who had lived in the same
basement apartment on NW Couch since 1987 and had arrangements the Night
considered under their protection.

The Church Network confirms involvement in two of the three cases. The third
they do not account for. [Gerald Fitch](npcs/gerald-fitch) has his suspicions
about who is responsible for the third and has not shared them.
