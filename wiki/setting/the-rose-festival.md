## The Rose Festival

Every June. Parades, dragon boats, a hundred thousand people on the waterfront
for the fireworks. The city at its most civic, most legible, most officially
itself.

Also: the single most contested supernatural political moment of the year.

The Rose Festival Foundation has run the event since 1907. That is not
coincidental. The Foundation understood, by the 1940s at the latest, that the
Festival generated something -- attention, repetition across generations,
flowers blooming in volume across the city -- that concentrated power in ways
the factions needed to negotiate around. The Festival is now both the city's
biggest public event and its most important supernatural political summit. The
two functions have never been separated.

---

## The Logistics

The Festival runs for three weeks, anchored by two major weekends. Grand Floral
Parade on the first Saturday: the largest all-floral parade in the world. Dragon
boat races on the Willamette the following weekend. Fleet Week runs concurrently
-- naval vessels docked at the waterfront, which the Church Network has opinions
about.

The Rose Festival Queen is selected annually from among high school students in
the Portland metro area. There are currently twenty-three princesses, one per
school district. The Queen is announced at the Grand Coronation.

This is the public-facing structure. It is accurate. It is also incomplete.

---

## What Concentrates

Something about the Festival accumulates across years. The repetition of it --
same streets, same flowers, same crowds at the same waterfront -- builds a kind
of pressure. The wild roses bloom harder during Festival week. The Willamette
moves differently at night, or seems to. The Wild's presence in the city is more
diffuse during the Festival and more focused after, as though the attention
disperses it and then draws it back.

No one has a satisfying explanation. The Foundation's Archive has three decades
of observational notes on this phenomenon and no conclusions. The Night
describes it as the city remembering itself. The Wild does not comment.

What the factions agree on: standing at the Festival table matters. Which
factions have visible presence during the event, which representatives attend
which ceremonies, who sits where during the Grand Floral Parade -- these details
are negotiated starting in February and the negotiations are not friendly.

## The Negotiation Structure

February: Foundation board meets in closed session to establish the year's
agenda. [Thomas Aldrich](npcs/thomas-aldrich) chairs this meeting. Faction
representatives are not present.

March: Informal channel meetings begin. The Foundation's liaison (currently the
board's third-longest-serving member) conducts separate conversations with Night
and Wild representatives. Power's interests are represented by the board itself.
Mortality's various communities are not formally invited and manage their
interests through other means.

May: Terms are finalized. What "finalized" means in practice is that the parties
have agreed not to openly disrupt the event. Side negotiations continue through
the Festival itself.

The Church Network attends the Festival publicly as civilians. They consider the
supernatural negotiation structure an abomination and do not participate in it.
They maintain surveillance on the waterfront during Festival week. They have
intervened twice in the past fifteen years. Both interventions are documented in
[the Archive](locations/the-archive).

## The Rose Festival Queen

The Queen is selected through a legitimate civic process: applications,
interviews, community service criteria. The Foundation does not manipulate the
selection. They have not needed to.

In the city's supernatural geography, the Queen occupies a position that was not
designed and is not fully understood. The title has existed since 1907. The
ritual of coronation -- flowers, public presentation, the specific waterfront
location -- has been stable for over a century. Something has accumulated in
that repetition.

The Queen for the duration of her reign has a quality the Wild recognizes and
the Night respects. She moves through contested space without difficulty. Things
that would trouble other people in those spaces do not trouble her. She does not
know this. Former queens do not know this either -- it fades when the title
passes, and the memory of those months is not precise.

The Foundation knows. They have known since at least the 1960s. They consider it
a management resource and have never explained it to anyone outside the board.

## After the Crowds Leave

During Festival week, the waterfront empties after the last night's fireworks.
The cleanup crews come in the morning. In between, something happens on the
Willamette.

The unhoused community does not go near the waterfront during Festival week.
When asked why, they say they have somewhere else to be.

What the Night does on the water during that window is not recorded anywhere
accessible. The Foundation's Archive has a section labeled FESTIVAL / RIVER and
it is one of the three sections with a secondary lock. The primary lock opens
with a board member's key. The secondary lock opens with something else.
