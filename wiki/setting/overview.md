## The Pitch

Portland sells itself as weird, welcoming, green. That's the mask.

Underneath: a city with bones stained by exclusion laws that kept Black
residents out for a century, a river that has swallowed secrets, and a Forest
Park that has been something's territory since before Lewis and Clark stumbled
through. The roses that give the city its name grow wild here. They don't need
tending. Nobody knows why. Nobody asks anymore.

City of Roses is a game about the people caught in the machinery of a city that
chews through lives quietly, officially, and with excellent branding. Power here
is old and patient. The dark is deep. And the deals that keep everything from
falling apart are older than anyone sitting at the table.

---

## Tone

Dark. Slow-burn. Political in the oldest sense -- who owes whom, who knows what,
who disappears when it becomes inconvenient.

This is not a city of open monster attacks. It's a city of missing persons
reports that go nowhere, city council votes that don't make sense, and
journalists who were very close to something and then very suddenly weren't. The
horror is systemic. The intrigue is the point.

Portland's progressive reputation is part of the texture -- it makes the rot
harder to see. People here believe they live somewhere good. That belief is a
resource the factions exploit.

---

## The Rose Festival

Every June. Parades, dragon boats on the Willamette, fireworks over the
waterfront. A hundred thousand people celebrating nothing in particular.

It is also the single most contested supernatural political moment of the year.
Something about the attention -- the ritual of it, the repetition across
generations, the flowers -- concentrates power in ways no one fully understands.
Every faction wants standing at the table. Every faction has leverage. The
negotiations start in February and they are not friendly.

---

## The Disappearances

Portland has been quietly bleeding people for years. Not deaths --
disappearances. The official explanation is the housing crisis. The real
explanation is contested. Every faction has a theory. Some of those theories are
self-serving. Some of them are right.

The unhoused community in Old Town keeps its own count, passed mouth to ear. It
is significantly higher than any official figure.
