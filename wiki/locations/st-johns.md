## St. Johns

North Portland's most distinct neighborhood, perched on a peninsula between the
Willamette and the Columbia Slough. Separated from the rest of the city by the
industrial corridor, it developed its own civic culture and still feels like a
separate town. Cathedral Park sits under the St. Johns Bridge -- gothic towers,
green lawn, the bridge's arches framing the river.

Faction: Mortality (Church Network). Primary operational base.

---

## The Bridge

The St. Johns Bridge is one of Portland's landmarks -- a green suspension bridge
with gothic towers, opened 1931. It is very beautiful. It is also a place where
people jump. The city has studied barrier installation multiple times. The
studies are inconclusive. The Church Network has its own explanation for the
bridge. They have not shared it with the city.

Cathedral Park under the bridge is where the Church Network holds its less
formal gatherings. The park is public. The gatherings look like picnics. They
are not picnics.

---

## The Network Here

Three churches anchor the St. Johns network: an evangelical congregation in a
converted auto dealership on Lombard, a smaller Pentecostal church that meets in
a storefront, and a home fellowship that doesn't have a fixed address and moves
every few months. The three groups are not formally affiliated. They share a
phone tree and a set of convictions about what is happening in the city.

The auto dealership pastor, a man named Gerald Fitch, is the closest thing the
network has to a coordinator. He is 63. He has been doing this for thirty years.
He is very tired and has no intention of stopping.

---

## The Slough

The Columbia Slough runs along the northern edge of St. Johns -- industrial,
unglamorous, ecologically complicated. The Church Network avoids it after dark.
They don't explain why to newer members until those members have been around
long enough to be trusted with the explanation. The explanation involves
something the Network encountered in the slough in 2011. Three members did not
come back from that encounter. The Network considers the slough the Night's
northern territory and treats the boundary accordingly.
