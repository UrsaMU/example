## Forest Park

5,100 acres on the ridge above Portland's west side. The largest urban forest in
the United States. It runs eight miles along the Tualatin Mountains, accessible
from dozens of trailheads, nominally managed by Portland Parks and Recreation.

Faction: Wild.

---

## The Park That Manages Itself

The official trail map shows 80 miles of maintained paths. The trails that
matter are not on the official map. Rangers who have worked the park for years
learn, without being told, which areas of the park they don't need to inspect.
Equipment that gets left in certain zones fails to start the next morning. The
paperwork explaining these patterns is voluminous and explains nothing.

Portland Parks employees have an informal orientation tradition for new Forest
Park staff. It is not written down.

---

## The Boundary

The park's eastern edge runs along a series of residential streets in northwest
Portland -- quiet, wealthy, with excellent views. Those houses are very
desirable. Their owners turn over more frequently than comparable properties
elsewhere in the city. The realtors have a phrase for it: "active neighborhood."
The phrase does not mean what it sounds like.

The boundary is where the city's interests and the Wild's interests negotiate.
It is not always a polite negotiation.

---

## The Eco-Resort Proposal

A development group backed by a California investment firm filed a proposal
three years ago for a boutique eco-resort in the park's southern section --
twelve cabins, a trail system, a "forest bathing" spa facility. The proposal has
been in the city planning review process since filing. It has not advanced.
Several commissioners who were initially supportive have quietly withdrawn their
backing.

The project manager, a woman named Rachel Voss, was reported missing eight
months ago. She is listed as a voluntary adult departure. Her colleagues believe
she walked away from the project under pressure. Her family does not believe
this.

The lead investor recently acquired a site in the Columbia River Gorge instead.
He has not explained the pivot. He has not returned calls from his original
partners.
