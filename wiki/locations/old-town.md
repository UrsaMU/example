## Old Town/Chinatown

The historic dark heart of Portland. The city's oldest neighborhood and its most
reliably ungovernable one. Every wave of urban renewal has broken against it.
Developers arrive with plans and leave without explanation. City initiatives are
announced and quietly forgotten. The neighborhood absorbs official attention and
returns nothing.

Faction: Night. The seat of the Night faction's power in Portland.

---

## The Street

Burnside marks the southern edge. The Pearl District is pushing north from the
other side. In between: bail bondsmen, missions, shelters, bars that have been
open since Prohibition in various configurations, a light rail stop that nobody
loiters near after dark without a reason.

The unhoused community here is not transient -- many have been in this
neighborhood for years, even decades. They know its geography at a level the
city has never mapped. They know which doorways are safe. Which blocks to avoid
on which nights. They know the arrangement with the Night, even if they have
never spoken it aloud.

---

## The Underground

Portland has extensive underground spaces -- old basements, utility tunnels, the
remnants of the Shanghai tunneling system that tourists are told is mostly
legend. In Old Town, some of those spaces are in use. By whom, for what, is not
a question the unhoused community answers directly. They just know which grates
not to open.

---

## The Arrangement

The unhoused community is not prey in Old Town. That is the deal. It is not
written. It has never been spoken between parties who could formalize it. It
holds because both sides need it to. The Night needs people who don't ask
questions and who notice strangers. The unhoused need a neighborhood where other
predators stay out.

It is more fragile than it looks. The gentrification pressure from the Pearl is
changing the demographics on the street. New faces mean the arrangement has to
be renegotiated, silently, one person at a time.
