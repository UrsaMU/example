## Cathedral Park

Under the St. Johns Bridge. A long green lawn running down to the Willamette,
framed by the bridge's gothic stone towers. The city maintains it. It has picnic
tables, a boat ramp, a small parking area. On summer weekends it is full of
families.

The Church Network holds its less formal gatherings here. The gatherings look
like picnics. They are not picnics.

Faction: Mortality (Church Network). Regular gathering site.

---

## The Bridge Above

The St. Johns Bridge opened in 1931 -- a green suspension span with gothic
towers, designed by David Steinman. It is one of Portland's most photographed
landmarks. It is also a place where people jump.

The city has commissioned barrier studies multiple times. Each study produces
inconclusive recommendations. The barriers are not installed. The Church Network
has its own explanation for the bridge's suicide rate. They have not shared it
with the city or with any journalist.

The explanation involves the bridge's position in the Night's territorial map.
The Slough is three miles east. Old Town is seven miles south. The bridge sits
between two Night-adjacent territories in a way that the Network believes
creates a kind of pressure at certain times of year. They cannot quantify this.
They have not tried to. Some things in the Network's operational knowledge exist
as pattern recognition, not theory.

[Gerald Fitch](npcs/gerald-fitch) has been to the bridge's edge twice in thirty
years on calls involving people who were about to jump. Both times, the person
he talked down was not in a state consistent with ordinary depression. Both
times, the thing that had been with them was gone by the time Fitch arrived. He
has a section in his private journal about this. It is one of the longer
sections.

## The Gatherings

The Network's Cathedral Park meetings are informal by design. No fixed schedule.
Attendance by phone-tree invitation. The group that meets here is the St. Johns
contingent -- the three congregations Fitch coordinates -- plus occasional
members from the Outer SE network who drive up for specific discussions.

The meetings cover: intelligence on Old Town and the Slough boundary,
coordination of ongoing operations, review of recent encounters, and what the
Network calls "discernment" -- collective assessment of which targets warrant
action and which should be monitored.

Discernment is the part of the process where errors get made. The intelligence
is often incomplete. The Network's operational doctrine does not require
certainty. It requires sufficient evidence of supernatural threat and collective
consensus to act. The standard for "sufficient" varies by who is in the group
that day and what has happened recently.

Fitch chairs the discernment discussions. He is the most cautious voice in them.
He does not always prevail.

## The Park Itself

Cathedral Park has a quality the Network members notice and do not discuss with
outsiders. The gothic arches of the bridge's underside -- the stone structures
that give the park its name -- create an acoustic space. Sound under the bridge
behaves differently. Voices carry at odd angles. Conversations that should be
private are not.

The Network uses this. They know which positions under the arches allow a
normal-volume conversation to be heard twenty feet away and which positions are
genuinely isolated. The layout of where people stand during meetings is not
accidental.

Non-Network civilians sometimes report the same acoustic strangeness and
attribute it to the bridge's architecture. They are not wrong about the
architecture. They are not fully right about the cause.

## Recent Activity

The Network has used Cathedral Park for three operational planning meetings in
the past six months. All three meetings addressed the Old Town situation --
specifically, the question of the Night's response to the missing three and how
far the Network was willing to push.

The third meeting, six weeks ago, reached a conclusion that Fitch considers
premature. He did not block it. He recorded his objections and they were noted.
The operation the meeting authorized has not yet been executed.

He is waiting for something to resolve. He is not sure what.
