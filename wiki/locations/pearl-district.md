## Pearl District

Portland's showcase neighborhood. Twenty years ago it was warehouses and rail
yards. Now it's condos, galleries, restaurants with wait lists, and the highest
property values in the city. Power did this deliberately. It is very proud of
the result.

Faction: Power. Primary civilian-facing territory.

---

## What It Is Now

Glass towers. A Whole Foods. Public art that was approved by committee. Dog
parks. The kind of neighborhood that appears in national magazine spreads about
Portland. The people who live here believe they chose it freely. Some of them
are right.

The Pearl is where Power puts its visible face -- the philanthropic galas, the
arts patronage, the civic leadership. It is the cleanest version of what
Portland's old money wants the city to be: controlled, prosperous, legible.

---

## The Northern Edge

The Pearl ends, officially, at Burnside. That is also where Old Town begins. The
buffer zone between them has historically been respected by both sides -- not
out of goodwill, but because both sides found the ambiguity useful. The Night
keeps the Pearl's worst problems from spilling south. Power keeps the city's
redevelopment appetite pointed away from Old Town.

That arrangement is under strain. Development money is moving north. New
construction is approaching the boundary in ways that the sixty- year-old
agreement between the factions didn't anticipate. Both sides are reading the
agreement's language carefully. Neither side wants what comes after negotiation
fails.

## The Thorn Has a Source Here

The Portland Thorn's Foundation source is, according to available intelligence,
somewhere inside Pearl District's civic infrastructure. The Foundation knows
this. It has not yet identified who. The source has been feeding documents for
eight months. At some point the Foundation will stop looking and start removing
candidates.
