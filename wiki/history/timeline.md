## Timeline

Two tracks. The mundane record and the hidden one. They have been running
parallel since before the city had a name. Where they intersect, someone usually
made sure the official record said something else.

---

## Before Portland

**Pre-contact** (no date) Mundane: Chinook and Multnomah peoples have inhabited
the confluence of the Willamette and Columbia for thousands of years. The area
is significant as a trading hub.

Hidden: The Wild's presence in the Tualatin Mountains predates human habitation.
Early oral traditions among river peoples acknowledge beings in the forest that
are not animals. These beings have protocols for the river boundary that are
older than any written record. The Night has no origin point that anyone can
fix.

---

**1792-1805** Mundane: British and American traders map the Columbia River.
Lewis and Clark overland expedition passes through the confluence.

Hidden: The expedition's private journals, not published, include one account of
a night camp in which something walked through camp without being seen. Clark's
entry dismisses it. The entry exists in the National Archives. Nobody has
written it up.

---

## The City Takes Shape

**1845** -- Portland platted by Asa Lovejoy and Francis Pettygrove. Name
selected by coin flip.

Hidden: The original land claim includes a notation, in Pettygrove's hand, about
"the old growth to the west" being excluded from development plans "by strong
prior arrangement." The notation is unattributed. It is in the Oregon Historical
Society collection. Nobody has asked the right questions about it.

---

**1857** -- Oregon state constitution includes explicit exclusion clauses
barring Black residents from settling. The laws persist in various forms
until 1926.

Hidden: The exclusion laws reshape Old Town's demographics in ways that benefit
the Night's territorial stability. This is not believed to be coincidental. The
Night's position in the subsequent decades solidifies around a neighborhood that
has been deliberately made inhospitable to outside scrutiny.

---

**1880s-1910s** -- Portland's Gilded Age. Old money consolidates. The families
who will anchor the [Power faction](factions/power) establish themselves.

Hidden: The Night makes its first documented formal agreements with human
institutional power during this period. The agreements are not archived anywhere
accessible. Their existence can be inferred from the behavior of city governance
around Old Town in the subsequent decades.

---

**1907** -- Rose Festival Foundation incorporated.

Hidden: Three of the seven founding board members are subsequently identified,
in internal Foundation documents from the 1940s, as having had "full awareness
of the city's nature." What they built the Festival to do is still the subject
of internal Foundation debate.

---

## The Twentieth Century

**1930s-1940s** -- Portland's industrial expansion. Vanport constructed north of
the city for wartime workers.

Hidden: The Night consolidates its underground infrastructure during this
period. The Shanghai tunnel network, largely abandoned by legitimate uses,
becomes something else.

---

**1943** -- The Foundation's Archive begins systematic documentation.

Hidden: A board member, returning from wartime service, convinces the board that
management of the supernatural requires institutional memory. The Archive is
established. The board member's name is not publicly associated with this
decision. His granddaughter currently sits on the board. She has read
approximately half the Archive.

---

**1948** -- Vanport Flood. The Columbia River dike fails; the entire city of
18,000 is destroyed in hours.

Hidden: The Foundation's records on the Vanport Flood run 40 pages. The official
cause was dike failure. What the Archive records about that night is in the
secondary-lock section. The entry is the longest single document in the Archive.
Three board members who have read it declined to summarize its contents at the
subsequent meeting.

---

**1948-1950** -- Foundation Board decision: manage, not expose.

Hidden: The board convenes three times in closed session following Vanport. They
reach a consensus that has governed Foundation policy since: the existence of
the supernatural in Portland is not to be disclosed publicly. The city will be
managed around it. This decision is documented in the Archive and referenced in
the Foundation's incorporation bylaws under a provision whose purpose is not
explained in public filings.

---

**1950s-1960s** -- Urban renewal destroys much of the historically Black Albina
neighborhood for the Memorial Coliseum and I-5.

Hidden: The Wild faction's response to the I-5 construction through the west
hills is documented in the Archive as "sustained territorial pressure, resolved
by buffer agreement." The specific buffer terms are not in the accessible
section.

---

**1959** -- City beautification project removes significant wild rose growth in
the NW district.

Hidden: See the Archive entry. Three weeks of incident concentration. The
incidents are in the secondary-lock section.

---

**1976** -- The Willamette River cleanup begins in earnest following EPA
designation.

Hidden: The cleanup reveals several things the sediment had been covering. The
Foundation's records from this period include a three-page memo titled RIVER
ASSESSMENT CONCERNS. It was circulated to four board members and then collected.
No copies are known to exist outside the Archive.

---

**1988** -- Reed College biology dissertation on wild rose growth patterns.
Paper not published; candidate transfers programs.

Hidden: See [The Wild Roses](setting/the-roses) for the full account. The
Foundation's role in suppressing the dissertation is documented internally. The
board member who recommended revisions has since died.

---

## Recent History

**2001** -- Night/Power border agreement formalized.

The 2001 agreement is the "sixty-year-old agreement" currently being disputed --
a miscount by the Power faction's legal team that has since become the standard
reference. The agreement actually dates from 2001, not 1961, though there was a
prior informal agreement from the earlier period. The distinction matters for
the lawyering. The Night knows this. They have not corrected Power's lawyers.

---

**2007** -- The Portland Thorn founded by a group of journalists leaving the
alt-weekly Willamette Week.

Hidden: Two of the founding journalists had followed a story in 2006 that
brought them into contact with Foundation suppression operations. They didn't
understand what they encountered. It gave the Thorn a persistent instinct toward
the kind of story they cannot yet name.

---

**2009** -- Wild rose growth documented at an unspecified location. Archive
entry reads: _the river does not forget._

---

**2011** -- The Slough Incident.

Mundane: Three members of a north Portland evangelical church community reported
missing in October. Reported to police. Listed as voluntary adult departures
within 60 days.

Hidden: The Church Network sent a team into the Columbia Slough following
intelligence that something had taken up residence there. Three members did not
come back. The Network established the slough as Night territory and has treated
the boundary accordingly since. What is in the slough is not on the Night's
territory map that was presented in the 2001 agreement. This is a point of
ongoing dispute.

---

**2019** -- Foundation kills a Portland Thorn story on city contracts.

Mundane: The Thorn investigates a story about irregularities in city contracts
connected to a 2018 Pearl District development. The story is killed after the
managing editor receives a call from a Foundation board member. The journalist
on the story leaves the Thorn.

Hidden: The story was three weeks from connecting Foundation activity to the
supernatural management structure. This is the closest the press has gotten. The
Foundation considers it a near-miss. They have a monitor on the Thorn's current
investigations.

---

**2022** -- Church Network feeds the Thorn a deliberate false lead.

The Thorn was developing a source in the outer SE evangelical community. The
Network identified the source and replaced the information flow with deliberate
misinformation. Two Thorn sources burned. The Thorn-Church relationship is
destroyed.

---

**2023, September** -- Rachel Voss disappears.

[Rachel Voss](npcs/rachel-voss), project manager for the Forest Park eco-resort
development, is reported missing. Listed as voluntary adult departure. Her
family does not accept this classification.

---

**2024** -- Three Night-affiliated individuals disappear from Old Town.

The Church Network confirms involvement in two cases. The third is unaccounted
for. The Night is nearly done collecting information before it acts.

---

**2024, October** -- A Portland Thorn source inside the Foundation begins
passing documents.

The source has been in contact with the Thorn for eight months. The documents
are pointed toward the disappearance data. The Thorn has not yet connected the
thread. They think they are working a corruption piece.

---

**Now** -- Current tensions at a point of convergence.

Pearl District expansion north. The eco-resort project stalled. The Night
preparing to act on the missing three. The Thorn close to something. The
Foundation watching.

Something is going to move.
