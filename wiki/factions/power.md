## Power

Old Portland money and the political machines it bought. Families who have been
in this city since its founding and have never released anything they touched.
Respectable. Civic-minded. Absolutely ruthless about the shape of the city's
future.

Power does not rule Portland overtly. It shapes the conditions in which Portland
governs itself. Zoning boards. Foundation boards. University endowments. The
police bureau command structure. The list of institutions they don't touch is
shorter than the list they do.

---

## Old Money vs New Money

Portland's tech boom brought new money. Power has spent fifteen years slowly
absorbing it, co-opting it, or redirecting it. The tech class believes it is
disrupting Portland's power structure. It is, instead, being digested. A few
have noticed. Most haven't.

---

## The Rose Festival

Power's most public-facing asset is its relationship with the Rose Festival
Foundation. The Foundation is technically independent. In practice, old money
families have sat on its board since 1907. The Festival is where Power displays
itself annually -- its civic virtue, its permanence, its generosity. It is also
where the supernatural political negotiations happen, and Power intends to
remain at that table.

---

## Current Pressure

The Pearl District push north is the most visible current move -- development
money flowing into territory adjacent to Old Town, not because the real estate
is especially valuable, but because moving that boundary changes the geometry of
every agreement the factions have. Some in Power know exactly what they're
doing. Some are civilians who are being used.

The Forest Park eco-resort project has stalled. The development team attributes
this to permitting delays. Two of the project's principals privately believe
something is discouraging progress. They are right.
