## Night

The old things. The Night faction encompasses vampires, ghosts, the hungry dead,
and the things that live in the margins of the light economy. Old Town/Chinatown
is their seat -- the one neighborhood in Portland that has never successfully
been made safe, legible, or profitable. That is not an accident.

The Night does not make war openly. It has survived long enough to know that
patience is a weapon. It makes deals. It collects debts. It waits.

---

## Internal Structure

The Night is not monolithic. It has no formal hierarchy. What it has is a set of
individuals with enough age, relationship, and mutual interest to act in rough
coordination when coordination is required. Most of the time it is not required.
Most of the time each operates independently in their own territory and
relationships, and the faction is more of a diplomatic category than an
organization.

Three figures define the Night's current position in Portland:

**[Eleanor Vance](npcs/eleanor-vance)** -- The senior figure. In Portland
since 1887. Has participated in every significant faction negotiation since the
1940s. The Night's institutional memory. Her position: the agreements hold
because everyone has more to lose from their failure than their maintenance. She
is beginning to question whether this calculation still applies.

**[Marcus Cole](npcs/marcus-cole)** -- The operational figure. In Portland
since 1971. Runs the Night's intelligence network in Old Town, manages
relationships with other factions at the working level, and handles the things
Eleanor considers beneath her direct attention. His position: patience is
failing and the Night needs to act before the Church Network understands how far
it can push.

**[Ida Blackwell](npcs/ida-blackwell)** -- The ghost. Technically neutral.
Died 1934. Known to every faction and deferred to by all of them on matters of
historical record and precedent. She is not a Night operative. She is something
the Night has chosen to maintain a relationship with. The distinction is
meaningful to her; it is not always meaningful in practice.

## Old Town/Chinatown

The historic dark heart of Portland. Every wave of urban renewal and civic
improvement has broken against it. Developers arrive confident and leave
quietly. City initiatives are announced and then are never mentioned again. The
neighborhood absorbs attention and returns nothing.

The unhoused community lives here in an arrangement with the Night that neither
side has ever formalized. The arrangement holds because both sides need it to.
It is more fragile than it looks.

---

## Current Position

The Pearl District expansion north and east is the Night's most pressing
political problem. Power's money is moving the boundary that has historically
kept the factions separated. The agreement that defined that boundary is sixty
years old. Both sides are lawyering the language. Neither side wants the
alternative to negotiation.

Three Night-affiliated individuals have gone missing in the last year. The
Church Network won't confirm or deny involvement. The Night is collecting
information before it acts. It is nearly done collecting.

---

## The Willamette

The Night has an interest in the Willamette that they have never fully explained
to the other factions. When asked, Eleanor describes the river as a record. She
does not clarify what it records.

What this means in practice: Eleanor is present on the waterfront during
Festival week, after the crowds leave. Ida Blackwell is present with her.
Whatever happens on the water during that window is not observed by anyone they
do not permit. The Foundation has tried twice to document it. Both attempts
produced equipment failure and partial memory gaps in the observation teams.

The Night's claimed interest in the river has never been formalized in any
faction agreement. It is treated as understood. The Foundation's board has
discussed whether to press for formalization three times in the last decade.
Each time, Thomas Aldrich has recommended patience. His reasons for this
recommendation are in the Archive.

---

## The Three Missing

Three Night-affiliated individuals have disappeared from Old Town in the last
year: a bartender with eleven years at a bar on NW Davis, a locksmith who ran
his business from a van, and a woman who had lived in the same basement
apartment on NW Couch since 1987.

The Night holds the Church Network responsible for two of the three. The third
is unaccounted for by any party who has been asked. Eleanor has a suspicion
about the third case. Marcus has a different suspicion. Neither has told the
other what they suspect.

The Night is nearly done collecting information before it acts. It has been
nearly done for eight months.

---

## What They Want

Stability. The agreements to hold. Old Town to remain theirs. For the city to
continue looking away. They have survived by being uninteresting to power. The
gentrification pressure is making that harder to maintain.

Marcus Cole would add: for the patience strategy to mean something, some
patience has to end eventually. He has not said this to Eleanor directly. He has
said it to Ida Blackwell. She did not tell him he was wrong.
