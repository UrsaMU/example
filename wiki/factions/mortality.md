## Mortality

Mortality is the human world pushing back -- people who know enough to be
dangerous, or are about to find out. In City of Roses, Mortality is not unified.
It is four communities with incompatible goals, incomplete information, and a
shared problem: everything else at the table is older, stranger, and more
patient.

Their internal tensions are as dangerous as anything the other factions can
bring to bear.

---

## The Four Communities

### The Press -- Expose it.

The Portland Thorn, a scrappy alt-weekly (founded 2009). Secular, pragmatic,
driven by story and the increasingly desperate economics of independent
journalism. They think they're working a corruption piece on city contracts.
Their source keeps turning up dead, relocated, or recanting. They are closer to
the truth than anyone has been in twenty years. They don't know this yet.

Wants: Publication. Accountability. A story that can't be killed. Fears: That
the story is too big to survive telling. Leverage: A source inside the Rose
Festival Foundation feeding documents for eight months.

---

### The Foundation -- Contain it.

The Rose Festival Foundation, incorporated 1907. Publicly: a civic organization
that runs Portland's signature annual event. Privately: the longest-running
institutional memory of the supernatural in the city. They have known since the
1940s. Their response was not to fight or expose -- it was to manage. Eighty
years of management has made them complicit in things they can no longer look at
directly.

Wants: The lid stays on. The Festival happens. The agreements hold. Fears: That
the agreements are already broken and they are the last to know. Leverage: Deep
ties to city government, police command, and every major institution in
Portland. Also: the Archive -- a locked room in their NW 19th Ave HQ, 80 years
of documented incidents, never shared with anyone.

---

### The Church Network -- Purge it.

No central organization. A phone tree. Three generations of outer-neighborhood
evangelical churches (Gresham, Milwaukie, outer SE, St. Johns). They do not
distinguish between a 200-year-old vampire who keeps the peace and a feral ghoul
that takes children. Everything supernatural is an abomination. They have done
things that would horrify the journalists. Some of those things were necessary.

Wants: A clean city. They know it's not achievable. They pursue it anyway.
Fears: Schism -- it is constant. And the possibility that some of what they've
killed deserved to live. Leverage: Numbers, geography (they operate where others
aren't), and operational security that comes from having no central location to
raid.

---

### The Unhoused -- Survive it.

Old Town's invisible population. No organization. Just hard-won knowledge passed
mouth to ear through a community that has been sleeping next to the supernatural
for decades. They've made accommodations -- some deeply uncomfortable -- to
exist in spaces the other factions don't know are contested. Most informed.
Least powerful. Some of the disappearances are theirs. Nobody is looking.

Wants: To survive. To not lose more people. To occasionally be treated as
something other than a resource or a problem. Fears: That the deal Old Town's
unhoused have with the Night is more fragile than it looks. Leverage: They see
everything. The river, the bridges, the underground, Old Town's back rooms --
maps in their heads that don't exist anywhere else.

---

## Tensions Between Communities

Press vs Foundation: Foundation killed their last major story (2019). Active
suppression ongoing.

Press vs Church: Church fed them a deliberate false lead (2022). Two sources
burned. Relationship destroyed.

Press vs Unhoused: Best sources, most endangered. Using them feels like
exploitation and probably is.

Foundation vs Church: Foundation sees them as dangerous amateurs. Church sees
Foundation as institutional accomplice.

Foundation vs Unhoused: Uses their invisibility operationally. Returns nothing.
Displaces them through development projects.

Church vs Unhoused: Some pastors do genuine outreach in Old Town. Some are
something else. Community is split on whether to trust any of them.
