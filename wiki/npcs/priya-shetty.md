## Priya Shetty

34. Co-founder and former CTO of a logistics software company acquired in 2022
    for $340 million. She moved to Portland in 2020, was invited onto the Rose
    Festival Foundation board in 2023, and has been on it for fourteen months.

She believes she is there to bring a fresh perspective to a legacy civic
organization. She is not wrong that she is bringing a fresh perspective. She is
wrong about what is happening to her.

---

## Before Portland

Priya grew up in Fremont, California. Stanford. Two startups that didn't work,
one that did. She came to Portland for the lifestyle, the housing prices
relative to the Bay Area, and a relationship that has since ended. She has
stayed because she likes the city and because leaving would feel like losing.

She is sharp. Her pattern recognition is good. Her technical background gives
her a systematic approach to problems. She is accustomed to being the person in
the room who sees what the others haven't. In her previous professional
contexts, this was accurate. In her current professional context, it is
approximately half accurate.

She sees the Foundation as a slightly sclerotic civic institution that needs
modernization. She is not wrong about the sclerosis. She has not yet understood
what the sclerosis is protecting.

---

## The Fourteen Months

In fourteen months, Priya has accomplished several things:

She pushed for the Foundation's financial records to be made more transparent.
[Thomas Aldrich](npcs/thomas-aldrich) agreed to a version of this that satisfied
her questions without touching the operational accounts. She considers this a
win.

She initiated a conversation about digitizing the Foundation's historical
records. Thomas expressed enthusiasm and appointed her to a subcommittee. The
subcommittee has met twice. The scope of what is to be digitized has been
narrowed each meeting. She is beginning to notice the narrowing.

She has been given access to the Foundation's offices on NW 19th. She has a key
to the building. Her key does not open the room at the end of the second-floor
corridor. She has asked about this room twice. She was told it is a storage room
containing materials under legal review. She accepted this. She is starting to
find the answer insufficient.

---

## What Is Happening to Her

Power absorbs new money. It has been doing this since Portland had money to
absorb. The tech class that came to Portland in the 2010s believed it was
disrupting the city's power structure. It was being digested.

Priya is being digested through the Foundation. The process is not coercive --
she is not being manipulated or controlled. She is being given access,
responsibility, and belonging. She is being made complicit, slowly, in the
decisions of an institution she does not fully understand. By the time she
understands it -- if she ever does -- she will have made enough decisions with
enough institutional consequence that leaving cleanly will be complicated.

She is not fully aware of this. She is 30% aware of it. She has a persistent
low-level unease about the Foundation that she has been attributing to a general
mid-30s restlessness.

---

## The Angle

Priya is the most accessible entry point into the Foundation for outside
parties. She answers her own email. She has coffee with people. She has not yet
learned which conversations she should not be having.

She is also, if she ever gets to the right questions, the person most likely to
actually push on the Archive. She is a systems thinker. She has noticed the
narrowing scope of the digitization project. She has a good memory for what she
has been told and a tendency to check whether answers given at different times
are consistent.

Thomas Aldrich has described her, in a conversation with a fellow board member:
_a problem that is also an asset, depending on how it develops._ He said this
without particular warmth or coldness. He is watching.
