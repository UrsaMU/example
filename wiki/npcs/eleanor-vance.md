## Eleanor Vance

Apparent age: mid-50s. Actual age: she arrived in Portland in 1887 and was not
young then.

The Night faction's senior figure in Portland. Not its ruler -- the Night does
not have rulers in any legible sense -- but the individual with the longest
continuous presence, the deepest institutional memory, and the most
relationships with the other factions. When decisions need to be made that will
outlast a human generation, the Night defers to Eleanor.

She does not call herself by any title. She is addressed, by the
Night-affiliated community and by the other factions who deal with her, simply
as Eleanor.

---

## What She Remembers

Eleanor was present for the original informal agreements between the Night and
Portland's early power structure in the 1880s-1900s. She was present for the
Rose Festival Foundation's founding in 1907. She participated in the 1940s
negotiations that produced the Foundation's management policy. She was at the
table for the 2001 formal border agreement.

She has a memory that encompasses the entire documented history of the city's
supernatural politics. She has outlasted every other participant in every
agreement she has made. This is, she has observed to
[Marcus Cole](npcs/marcus-cole) on at least one occasion, both an advantage and
a problem: she can enforce continuity, but she is also the only one who fully
understands what the original terms were and why, and when she is no longer here
that knowledge goes with her.

She has not done anything to record it. The Night does not keep written records.

---

## Her Position on the Current Tensions

Eleanor knows the 2001 agreement's actual text and the history behind it. She
knows that Power's lawyers are misrepresenting the agreement's origin date, and
she has not corrected them. She is letting the misrepresentation accumulate,
strategically.

She is less certain about the missing three. She knows the Church Network is
responsible for two of them. The third troubles her more than she has said to
anyone except [Ida Blackwell](npcs/ida-blackwell). Her suspicion about the third
case is not yet actionable.

She is inclined toward patience. She has always been inclined toward patience.
Marcus Cole thinks this inclination has become a liability. She does not
disagree as much as she once would have.

---

## Her Relationship to the River

Eleanor's interest in the Willamette is the Night's most consistent long-term
project and the one she will not explain. When the other factions ask what the
Night means by describing the river as a record, Eleanor gives a version of the
same answer: _the river holds what the city wants to forget. We keep the
account._ She does not elaborate.

What she actually does at the waterfront during Festival week, after the crowds
leave, is not observed by anyone she does not permit to observe it. The
Foundation has tried twice to document it. Both times the observation team
reported equipment failure and partial memory gaps.

---

## Day-to-Day

Eleanor operates through a property management company she has controlled, under
various names, since 1932. The company manages several buildings in Old Town,
including the building containing the bar on NW Davis whose bartender is among
the recent missing. She knows every tenant in every building she manages. She
knew the bartender for eleven years.

She does not meet in person with faction representatives except at the Festival
negotiations. Other contact is through intermediaries. The intermediaries are
not always people.

She is currently in communication with [Thomas Aldrich](npcs/thomas-aldrich)
about the Pearl District boundary question. The communications are civil. They
are not warm. They have both been at this long enough to know what warmth costs.
