## Thomas Aldrich

67. Third-generation Portland. His grandfather was on the Rose Festival
    Foundation board when the 1940s management policy was established. His
    father chaired the board through the 1980s. Thomas has chaired it
    since 2009.

He knows what the Foundation knows. He has read the Archive, in full, including
the secondary-lock sections. He has not shared its contents with his wife, his
children, or his personal attorney. He has shared precise summaries with three
other board members over fifteen years. He considers this appropriate
institutional discretion.

He sleeps fine. He has examined this fact about himself with some care.

---

## The Position He Holds

The Foundation's chair serves as the primary interface between the Foundation's
institutional function (managing Portland's supernatural political landscape)
and its public function (running a civic festival). Thomas is good at both. He
is better at the former.

He has been in direct contact with [Eleanor Vance](npcs/eleanor-vance) since
inheriting the board chair. Their relationship is managed, professional, and
characterized by a mutual understanding that they are both representing
institutions that need each other and do not fully trust each other. Eleanor's
memory of his grandfather is longer than Thomas's own memory. He finds this
useful. He also finds it, occasionally, uncomfortable.

He knows the Night's border dispute with Power's development interests is a
problem. He is not the faction that created the problem -- the development money
behind the Pearl District push north is not Aldrich family money. Thomas is one
of three board members attempting to manage the situation without the other
parties understanding that the Foundation is managing it.

---

## What He Wants

The lid to stay on. The Festival to happen. The agreements to hold.

He is the direct institutional heir to the 1948 management decision and he
believes, sincerely, that it was the correct call. The alternative --
disclosure, exposure, public knowledge of the supernatural -- he has modeled in
his mind many times. He does not think it ends well for the city, for the
factions, or for the humans who would be caught in the subsequent reckoning.

He is aware that this position conveniently aligns with the interests of
institutions he benefits from. He has examined this alignment. He has concluded
that it does not invalidate the position.

---

## What He Doesn't Know

The Foundation has a source inside its own organization communicating with the
Portland Thorn. Thomas does not know who it is. He knows a leak exists -- the
Thorn has information that could only come from Foundation documents. He has not
yet identified the source.

He has also not yet understood that the Thorn's investigation, which he believes
is a manageable press problem, is approaching something that his normal
suppression tools will not resolve. The previous kill in 2019 worked because the
journalist was operating alone and the story was incomplete. The current
investigation has a source with sustained document access. If it runs, it will
be different.

---

## Practical Details

Thomas's family money is in timber, real estate, and, since the 1990s, private
equity focused on Pacific Northwest infrastructure. He is genuinely wealthy in
ways that predate the tech economy. He is on the board of Portland General
Electric, Oregon Health & Science University, and two other civic institutions
he does not discuss.

He lives in a house in the West Hills that his grandfather built. He drives a
nine-year-old Volvo. He is not performatively humble about the money; he simply
does not think about it very much. It is a condition of his life, not a source
of identity.

He chairs the Festival negotiations. He is effective at it. He has managed
faction representatives who are older and stranger than anything in his official
professional experience with a steadiness that
[Eleanor Vance](npcs/eleanor-vance) has acknowledged, once, as impressive.

He considered this acknowledgment for several days afterward. He did not know
what to do with it. He still doesn't.
