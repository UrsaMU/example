## Gerald Fitch

63. Pastor of an evangelical congregation in a converted auto dealership on
    Lombard Avenue in St. Johns. The closest thing the
    [Church Network](factions/mortality) has to a coordinator.

He has been doing this for thirty years. He is very tired. He has no intention
of stopping.

---

## The Man

Gerald Fitch came to St. Johns in 1996 from a Pentecostal background in eastern
Oregon, following a calling he describes to his congregation as "a sense of what
the city needed." What he actually encountered in his first year -- and what
converted his abstract theological conviction into operational knowledge -- he
does not discuss publicly. He discussed it once, in 2003, with a Network
colleague who has since moved to Spokane. The colleague's account of that
conversation describes Fitch going very quiet and then saying: _I saw what was
on the other side of the deal and I understood what I was supposed to do._

He is not charismatic in the conventional sense. He is methodical. He runs the
phone tree personally, keeps the contact lists encrypted on a system he built
himself in 2015, and has maintained operational security across thirty years and
four different law enforcement inquiries without a significant breach. The other
Network coordinators in Gresham and Milwaukie consult him before acting. He does
not always answer immediately.

He attends the Rose Festival every year. He watches. He makes notes in a
physical journal he keeps at home, not in the church.

---

## What He Knows

After thirty years, Fitch's operational knowledge of Portland's supernatural
geography is probably the most comprehensive in the Mortality faction and
arguably more detailed in some respects than the Foundation's Archive.

He knows the Old Town boundary and its practical implications. He knows the
Slough is Night territory and what it cost the Network to learn that. He knows
the wild rose patterns in the northern neighborhoods and has his own theory
about what they mean that differs from the Wild faction's apparent position and
from the Foundation's documented observations.

He knows, or believes he knows, which businesses in Old Town have Night
connections. His list is approximately 70% accurate. The 30% error rate has
caused problems.

He knows that the Foundation exists and that it suppresses information. He does
not know the extent of the Archive. He has contempt for the Foundation's
management approach and considers their complicity in the supernatural's
continuance to be a moral failure of the first order.

He does not know that the Foundation has records suggesting some of what the
Network has killed over thirty years were not what the Network believed them to
be. He has not asked the right questions to find out.

---

## What He's Done

Three confirmed operations in Old Town in the past year. Two of them were
targeted at individuals the Night considers under their protection. One of them
Fitch considers unambiguously justified. The second he has more complicated
feelings about -- the intelligence was from a source that has proven unreliable
before, and he approved the operation anyway.

The third missing individual in Old Town -- the one not attributed to Network
operations by Fitch's accounting -- he has a suspicion about. He has not shared
it with anyone because confirming it would mean confronting the possibility that
someone in the Network is operating without authorization. Or something else is.

He has a list. He reviews it periodically. Some items on the list are
operations. Some are question marks. The question marks have been growing.

---

## Current Situation

Fitch is aware the Night is collecting information and preparing to act. He has
been expecting this for months. He is not certain whether the Network's current
position -- collect, wait, continue operating -- is sustainable or whether they
are approaching a confrontation they are not prepared for.

He is considering reaching out to an intermediary. He has not yet.

The auto dealership church holds services Tuesday and Sunday. Fitch's office is
in what used to be the service bay. He has a kettle, a folding table, and three
filing cabinets. The filing cabinets are locked. The locks are not the
originals.
