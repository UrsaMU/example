## Ida Blackwell

Died 1934. Has been in Old Town since approximately 1890, which means she was
present for most of her own life and has continued to be present for ninety
years after it ended. The distinction between what she remembers from life and
what she has accumulated since is not clear, to her or to anyone else.

Technically neutral. Every faction that operates in Portland defers to her on
questions of history and precedent. Nobody formally invited her to that role. It
accumulated.

---

## What She Is

Ida Blackwell is not precisely a ghost in the folkloric sense. She is not bound
to a location, does not haunt in any consistent pattern, and has been observed
in the Pearl District, Cathedral Park, the Hawthorne Bridge, and the waterfront
during Festival week -- not simultaneously, but in ways that suggest she moves
with a purpose that is not visible from outside.

What she was in life: a property records clerk for Multnomah County from 1908
until her death in 1934. She spent twenty-six years looking at who owned what,
who transferred what to whom, who was recorded and who was not. This expertise
did not end with her life. It expanded.

She knows the property history of Portland with the completeness that only the
records allow -- and she has continued to track it since 1934 in ways that the
records do not. She knows who sold what under pressure. She knows which
addresses have changed hands in patterns that don't make sense economically. She
knows the geography of the Night's territorial claims in property terms more
precisely than the Night's own maps.

---

## Her Relationship to the Factions

Everyone consults her. Nobody controls her or speaks for her. She decides who
she speaks to and when.

[Eleanor Vance](npcs/eleanor-vance) is the person Ida has talked to most
continuously over the longest period. The relationship is not friendship in any
legible sense -- it is more like two people who have been looking at the same
city from different angles for long enough to trust each other's observations.
Eleanor tells Ida things she tells no one else. Ida does not repeat them, except
when she judges it necessary.

[Marcus Cole](npcs/marcus-cole) she is observing closely. She told him once, in
terms that were not encouraging, that impatience in a creature with his kind of
time is usually about something other than what it appears to be. He has been
thinking about this.

The Foundation has tried three times to establish a regular channel with Ida.
She attended the first meeting, said nothing, and left. She did not attend the
subsequent ones.

[Gerald Fitch](npcs/gerald-fitch) has encountered her twice in thirty years.
Both times he did not understand what he was talking to. She did not clarify.

---

## The River

Ida's interest in the Willamette predates her death. She grew up near the river,
worked near it, died near it. What her relationship to it is now is not
something she has explained.

She is present at the waterfront during Festival week. She is present with
Eleanor. What happens there is not observed by parties they do not permit. Ida
has said, once, to a Thorn journalist who encountered her in 2018 without
understanding the encounter: _the river is the only honest record this city has.
Everything else has been revised._

The journalist included this quote, attributed to "an elderly woman near the
waterfront," in a piece about the Festival's history. The Foundation flagged the
piece. Ida has not been observed in contact with any Thorn staff since.

---

## How to Find Her

You don't. She appears when she has something to say or when she judges a
situation warrants her presence. The most reliable pattern: she tends to appear
within a few days of significant property transactions in Old Town -- especially
ones that involve addresses she has tracked for decades.

She has been seen twice in the past month near the blocks where the Pearl
District development is pressing north. She has not spoken to anyone during
these appearances. She is watching.
