## Rachel Voss

38 at time of disappearance. Project manager for the Forest Park eco-resort
development proposal. Reported missing September 2023. Listed as voluntary adult
departure.

---

## The Public Record

Rachel Voss joined the development firm Cascadia Land Partners in 2021, hired
specifically to manage the Forest Park proposal through Portland's permitting
process. She had prior experience in complex land-use projects in the Willamette
Valley and a reputation for staying calm in regulatory friction.

She was good at the job. The project's stall was not, her colleagues say, for
lack of effort on her part. She attended every planning commission session. She
built relationships with the parks bureau staff. She documented every obstacle
and every delay with the systematic patience of someone who had navigated
difficult projects before.

In August 2023, colleagues describe a change. She became difficult to reach. She
missed two internal meetings. When she did appear, she was described as
distracted, preoccupied, and -- by one colleague, in a conversation that was
later shared with her family -- "like she had seen something she was trying to
decide what to do about."

She was last seen September 14, 2023, leaving the firm's Pearl District office
at 6:47 PM. Her car was found two days later at a Forest Park trailhead on NW
Firelane 5. Her phone was in the car. Her laptop was not.

---

## What Her Colleagues Believe

The official firm position is that Rachel experienced a personal crisis and left
the project voluntarily. The development's lead investor subsequently pivoted to
a Columbia River Gorge site and stopped returning calls from the Portland
partners, which the firm has tried to attribute to coincidence.

Privately: the two project principals who remained in Portland after the
investor's departure believe Rachel was pressured out. By whom they are not
sure. The regulatory opposition to the project had been oddly consistent -- not
aggressive, just persistent, emerging from multiple uncoordinated-seeming
directions simultaneously. One of the principals described it, in a conversation
his wife later mentioned to a friend who mentioned it to the Thorn: "like the
city itself didn't want it to happen."

He meant this metaphorically. He was closer to accurate than he knew.

---

## What Her Family Believes

Rachel's family -- her mother in Lake Oswego, her brother in Seattle -- does not
accept the voluntary departure classification. They filed a missing persons
report. They hired a private investigator for four months. The investigator
found nothing actionable and the family could not sustain the cost.

Her mother has a website. It has Rachel's photo, a summary of her last known
movements, and a contact form. It has received eleven responses, three of which
were credible. One of those three came from an anonymous sender and described
seeing a woman matching Rachel's description in the Forest Park area "near the
old Leif Erikson trailhead, in October, talking to someone she couldn't see."
The sender did not leave contact information.

Her mother forwarded this to the Portland Police Bureau. They noted it and did
not follow up.

## Staff Notes -- What Actually Happened

Rachel Voss went into Forest Park following her own investigation. She had spent
three months quietly trying to understand why the project was being blocked. She
had found, in the public-facing environmental review materials, the wild rose
distribution map she had submitted -- and had noticed, by comparing it to
subsequent planning documents, that her data had been quietly modified in the
official record. The zones she had flagged as high-density wild rose growth had
been reclassified in the city's version.

She went to the park to verify her original survey. She was in the park after
dark on September 14th.

She encountered a Wild faction representative. The encounter was not violent. It
lasted approximately four hours, from available inference. What was communicated
during those four hours is not known.

Rachel Voss is currently alive. She is not in Portland. She has not contacted
her family. Whether this is her choice is a matter that the Wild faction
considers resolved and that her mother would contest if she knew there was
something to contest.

Her laptop is not in the park. Its location is unknown.

## Relevance to Current Play

Rachel's disappearance is one of three cases in the
[Disappearances](setting/the-disappearances) pattern with above-average
documentation. The Thorn's Foundation source has not pointed them toward Rachel
specifically -- the source's documents focus on the Old Town clustering -- but a
direct inquiry into the eco-resort project would get there.

The private investigator's case files are not destroyed. They are in a storage
unit in Beaverton.

The anonymous tip to Rachel's mother's website came from someone who knows more
than they said. They have not sent another message.
